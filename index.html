Francisco Pereira (1990, São Paulo) is a visual artist and art historian. His practice develops primarily through cyanotype, a photographic process activated by sunlight. Each of his series starts from a single matrix, which is manually reprinted many times, revealing a sequence of misalignments, movements, and the implacable effects of time. What emerges from this process are not exact reproductions, but shifting images that refuse to stay the same.
He has lived and worked in Barcelona since 2019. He completed a Master's in Art History at the University of Barcelona in 2021, and since 2022 has taken part in collective exhibitions and cultural mediation projects in the city, presenting works at La Capella (2022), Centre Cívic Torre Llobeta (2024 and 2025), and Magber Gallery (2026).
